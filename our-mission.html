OUR MISSION

Food waste in the United States has become a critical issue, with millions of pounds of edible produce discarded each year despite rising environmental, economic, and social costs.

In 2023, U.S. farms produced 17.6 million tons of surplus food, and 15.4 million tons of it ultimately went to waste.

In meal terms, the waste corresponds to about 28.8 billion meals.

This growing crisis not only strains natural resources but also widens gaps in community nutrition and access to fresh, healthy foods.

The EPA's "From Farm to Kitchen" report estimates that since 1960, U.S. food loss and waste (in supply chain + disposal) has tripled.

So how are we solving this?

WHAT WE DO

1

Collaborate with a local farm to rescue surplus produce that would otherwise be wasted.

2

Partner with a local school to integrate rescued produce into an educational lesson.

3

Facilitate a cooking session where students and parents prepare fresh, nutritious meals using the rescued produce.

4

Students practice cooking while learning about the impacts of food waste.

5

Encourages hands-on learning and quality time between students and their parents.

6

Provide students and parents with educational materials, such as children's books and cookbooks, to support ongoing learning and food waste reduction at home.

FARM WASTE

Farm food waste occurs long before produce reaches consumers, with millions of pounds of perfectly edible fruits and vegetables left unharvested each year because they don't meet buyer standards or aren't worth the cost of harvesting.

This early-stage waste represents a major loss of resources—land, water, labor, and energy—and highlights a critical gap in the food system where fresh, nutritious food is routinely lost before it ever has a chance to nourish communities.

EDUCATION

Studies show that early exposure to education about sustainability helps children build lifelong habits that support environmental responsibility and informed decision-making.

Learning about food systems, waste, and resource use at a young age encourages students to understand the impact of their choices on their communities and the planet.

HABITS

This foundation empowers them to grow into adults who value conservation, support sustainable practices, and contribute to healthier, more resilient systems.

ROOTS & ROUTES' take-home educational materials extend learning beyond the classroom, giving students and their families simple, practical ways to engage with sustainability together. By bridging school lessons with family routines, these materials help students build sustainable habits early while empowering families to adopt more mindful, waste-reducing practices at home.
